Lesson #1

How to create analog-like photos with your iPhone using free apps only?

  • Step 1 - Download apps

    During this lessson you can repeat all the steps with the source image. Let's get started! We're gonna need VSCO and Snapseed. You can instal both apps from the AppStore.

  • Step 2 - Filters

    Open Photos app on your iPhone, choose your photo and click on the icon. Choose "Import to VSCO". Then go to the app, tap on the thumbnail and choose "Edit Image".

    First we have to choose our Filter. For this example I used DOG3, which has nice reddish tint. It is perfect for our tomatos!

  • Step 3 - Exposure

    Filters aren't tailored for every photo, so we have to make a few adjustments. If our image is straight and we don't want to crop it to specific ratio, we can start from the Exposure. In general it can make our image brighter or darker.

    It was a sunny day, so I would like to have my photo a little overexposed, so I added +2.0. .

    TIP: Pay attention to the brightest and darkest part of your image. Making image brighter ensure that you don't lose details in the higlights.

  • Step 4 - Contrast & Saturation

    I like this pale analog look, but we can increase Contrast a little bit. Let's add +0.5 and go to the Saturation to make our tomatos stand out a little bit. Increase Saturation by +1.1 .

  • Step 5 - White Balance

    I feel like now photo looks too warm and there is too much red in the background. I can fix it with the White Balance.

    There are two settings - first - we can make our photo looks colder by decreasing Temperature by -2.0. Then I would like to get rid of this red tint a little bit, so I stepped back Tint also by -2.0.

    TIP: Tint is the balance between green and red. If the skin looks too red on the portrait, you can use Skin Tone instead.

  • Step 6 - Vignette

    before

    after

    For the final touch we can add Vignette. This effect was common in the vintage cameras. It is dark shadow or blur on the boarder of the image caused by the lens. It is nice effect if we want to focus attention in the middle of the frame. I used +12.0 (maximum).

  • Step 7 - Export from VSCO

    Good job! You finished your work in VSCO. Now you can export it to Photos app by tapping on "Next" and choosing "Save to Camera Roll and Draft". If you don't want to share your photo remember to uncheck "Post to VSCO".

    This is not the end yet! We would like to add some blur and grain to this photo to make it looks more vintage. Now open exported photo in Snapseed and go to another Step.

  • Step 8 - Blur

    Photos from digital camera have increased sharpness and they don't look like the ones from vintage camera. We can fix it by using Blur tool in Snapseed. Tap on the Tools in the bottom of the screen and choose Blur. Usually we use it to create a depth of field by preserving some part of the photo sharp. However this time we will use it in a different way.

    Using pinch-in shrink the circle to the lowest size. Decrease the amount of the Blur to 8 by sliding your finger left on the photo. Then drag you finger up to go to the Transition and set value to 0 by swiping left. Do the same with Vignette Strengh. Put the blue dot (center point of blur) in one of the corners like on the screenshot. Confirm change in the bottom right corner.

  • Step 9 - Grain

    after

    For the last step we use Grain in Snapseed to make the photo looks even softer. You can use Grain in VSCO, but it is only available in paid version. Let's set Grain to +35 and set the second parameter to 0. Voil√†! You can export final result.
